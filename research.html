Research

A research agenda organized for both specialists and adjacent readers

My work examines how employees succeed or struggle across professional, moral, and nonwork domains. The filters below are designed to help visitors quickly find projects that overlap with their interests, methods, or disciplinary lens.

Under Review

When employees rationalize small ethical compromises

Question: How do ordinary workplace pressures slowly reshape moral judgment?

This study investigates how repeated demands, norms, and situational cues can alter the way employees understand and justify ethically ambiguous action.

Why it matters: Small ethical shifts can accumulate into major organizational consequences.

Writing

How unfinished work spills over into life outside the office

Question: What makes some work demands especially hard to leave behind?

This project explores the mechanisms through which work intrudes on personal life, and how those mechanisms shape recovery, strain, and well-being.

Why it matters: Understanding spillover helps clarify the boundaries between work strain and human flourishing.

Data Analysis

Recognizing initiative before it becomes visible performance

Question: How can we better identify employees who are proactive in subtle but meaningful ways?

This work uses field evidence and AI-assisted annotation to surface indicators of initiative that may not appear in conventional performance metrics.

Why it matters: Organizations often reward visible outcomes while missing the behaviors that create them.

Model Brainstorming

Communication patterns that strengthen or strain workplace relationships

Question: Why do some difficult conversations build trust while others weaken it?

I examine how employees interpret communication signals and how those interpretations shape conflict, cooperation, and relational durability.

Why it matters: Interpersonal processes are often the path through which broader organizational dynamics become personal and consequential.
