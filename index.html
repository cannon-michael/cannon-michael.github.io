Research Focus

Scholarly, practical, and attentive to the full arc of employee life

I study the factors that help employees thrive or struggle across professional performance, moral conduct, and life beyond work. My goal is to produce research that is theoretically grounded, methodologically rigorous, and legible across management and adjacent behavioral disciplines.

This site is designed to make that work easy to navigate. Visitors can browse projects by topic area, data source, and method, then move quickly to the papers and collaborations most relevant to them.

Working Paper

When employees rationalize small ethical compromises

Hook: How do ordinary workplace pressures slowly reshape moral judgment?

This project examines how everyday demands and social cues influence employees’ willingness to justify ethically questionable behavior over time.

How unfinished work spills over into life outside the office

Hook: What makes some work stressors harder to leave behind than others?

Current Study

Recognizing initiative before it becomes visible performance

Hook: How can we better identify employees who are proactive in subtle but meaningful ways?

This work combines field evidence with AI-assisted annotation to detect behavioral signals of initiative that traditional performance indicators may miss.
